### HISTORY TEASER (short version on home page — full versions in history doc)

The history of Fryman Estates begins in the early 1930s, when Harry C. Fryman started
acquiring land along the northern side of Mulholland Drive. At the time, the area was largely
rural and undeveloped, consisting of open hillside terrain and canyon land. Fryman opened the
property to civic and charitable events, hosting large community gatherings that brought
public attention to the canyon's natural beauty.

Following Fryman's death in the 1940s, the area gradually transitioned from ranchland to
residential use. Over the coming decades, parcels were sold and developed into custom hillside
homes, many along what are now Fryman Road and Oakdell Road. Among the early property owners
was entertainer Gene Autry, whose involvement helped establish the neighborhood's reputation
as a secluded retreat for high-profile residents.

By the 1950s, homeowners formed a neighborhood improvement association to help preserve the
area's unique character. Their efforts extended beyond Fryman Estates, as they became founding
members of the Hillside Federation, a coalition advocating for responsible development across
the Santa Monica Mountains.

Throughout the years, residents of Fryman Estates have continued to play a central role in
defending the integrity of the neighborhood. They successfully opposed a plan by Chapman
College to build a campus on land now known as Laurelwood Estates, and later helped block a
proposal to extend the 170 Freeway through Laurel Canyon. That project aimed to connect the
101 and 90 Freeways, creating a direct route to LAX, but would have dramatically altered the
quiet, residential feel of the area. These preservation victories helped define the values
that continue to shape Fryman Estates today.

### NEIGHBORHOOD FEATURES — "Where Heritage Lives Beneath the Trees"

Fryman Estates is known for its peaceful setting, mature landscaping, and enduring sense of
character. Tree-lined streets wind through the hills, with homes often tucked behind gates and
framed by native vegetation. Towering oak and sycamore trees, some more than 400 years old,
create a natural canopy that defines the neighborhood's visual identity.

This lush hillside environment, combined with architectural variety and a strong sense of
privacy, makes Fryman Estates one of the most treasured residential enclaves in Studio City.

### ARCHITECTURAL HIGHLIGHTS — "Evolving Homes Rooted in Ranch Design"

Fryman Estates homes reflect a thoughtful architectural evolution that began with classic
ranch-style residences, many originally designed to follow the natural contours of the
hillside. Over time, these properties have been expanded or rebuilt to showcase a variety of
styles, including Mid-Century Modern, Traditional, and Contemporary architecture. The result
is a neighborhood with architectural diversity that remains cohesive and intentional.

Although each home is distinct in style, they share a focus on quality craftsmanship, privacy,
and a strong connection to the natural surroundings. Large lots, mature oak trees, and
well-designed layouts contribute to the lasting appeal of this hillside community.
